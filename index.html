Proposition: Filing a police complaint is worthless, as they lead to no actual justice or change.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • I used the width of the flow lines to indicate the quantity of complaints that went to each outcome. Thicker lines indicate that more complaints went to that outcome. By showing how many complaints are disregarded with a really thick line and how few complaints result in charges with a really thin line, I hope to convey how the majority of complaints are disregarded or result in minimal punishment. I dishonestly put extra emphasis on this by making all the flows to punitive outcomes even thinner than they should be and the other flows thicker. Score: -1
  • Area is not the most effective visualize channel, so I used color of the flow lines as a redundant encoding for the quantity of complaints that went to each outcome. Deeper blues indicate higher quantities. The deep blues make the thick flow lines look bolder, while the light blues make the thin flow lines look even thinner. I performed the same dishonest techniques with this encoding as I did with the flow line width. Score: -1
  • I used node placement on the x-axis to indicate how severe the complaint's resulting punishment is. The further right the node is, the more severe the punishment is. More severe punishments require more work and processes to achieve, which is represented through distance across the x-axis. Score: 2
  • I used color of the nodes as a redundant encoding for outcome severity. Deeper shades of red indicate more severe punishment. To highlight how being charged is the only actual severe outcome, I made the node for charges a very deep red, while all other nodes are yellow or orange. Score: 2
  • Some of the punishment names don't do a good job describing what the punishment is, like "command-level discipline". Most of these punishments aren't too substantial. For example the most severe instance of command-level discipline is losing ten vacation days. To show how most punishments are not very intense, I detailed each outcome in the legend. Score: 1.5

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • I used counts on the y-axis instead of proportions. Since the number of complaints being filed every year increases, the number of substantiated complaints increase as well. This makes the linear relationship between counts of substantiated complaints and year very clean and observable. Proportion of substantiated complaints also increases as time goes by, but the linear relationship is much weaker and noisier. Score: -1
  • I changed the aspect ratio of my graph to make it taller. This highlights the linear trend displayed within and makes it look steeper. This reinforces the idea that more and more complaints are being substantiated each year. Score: 0
  • I removed the 2019 and 2020 data from the dataset because they were not agreeing with the linear trend displayed for the other years. So, the graph only displays data from 1985 to 2018. Score: -2
